Cookies Security Commentary Research

Cookies play a critical role in web development for storing user session data, preferences, and authentication details. However, improper cookie handling in JavaScript can introduce significant security vulnerabilities.

Key security concerns include Cross-Site Scripting (XSS), where malicious scripts access cookies containing sensitive information. To mitigate this risk, cookies should be flagged as HttpOnly, restricting their access from JavaScript. Additionally, enabling the Secure flag ensures cookies are transmitted only over HTTPS, protecting them from interception.

Another vital measure is the SameSite attribute, which prevents cookies from being sent with cross-site requests, reducing the risk of Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF) attacks. Setting SameSite=Strict or SameSite=Lax provides effective protection based on application needs.

Lastly, sensitive data should never be stored in cookies in plain text. Instead, encryption and careful validation should be implemented to minimize risks, enhancing overall security.
